About Setter Gordon: Your Gordon Setter Resource

Our Mission and Purpose

Setter Gordon exists to provide accurate, comprehensive information about the Gordon Setter breed to prospective owners, current Gordon enthusiasts, and anyone interested in these distinguished Scottish sporting dogs. This breed deserves knowledgeable advocacy - Gordon Setters remain relatively rare with only a few hundred puppies registered annually in the United States, and misinformation about their needs and characteristics can lead to poor matches between dogs and families.

Our content focuses on the realities of Gordon Setter ownership, not romanticized versions that ignore the breed's substantial exercise requirements, training challenges, and grooming needs. We emphasize the importance of health testing, responsible breeding practices, and the commitment required to properly raise and maintain these athletic dogs. Too many sporting breeds end up in rescue situations because families underestimated their needs or selected based on appearance rather than lifestyle compatibility.

The information presented here draws from breed standards established by the Gordon Setter Club of America, veterinary research on breed-specific health concerns, field trial performance data, and the collective experience of breeders and owners who have dedicated decades to this breed. We aim to help potential owners make informed decisions about whether a Gordon Setter fits their lifestyle while supporting current owners with practical guidance on training, health management, and activities that fulfill their Gordon's working heritage. For more information, visit our home page or check out our FAQ section.

Understanding the Gordon Setter Community

The Gordon Setter community remains relatively small but deeply passionate about preserving this breed's working abilities and temperament. The Gordon Setter Club of America, founded in 1924, serves as the parent club and maintains breed standards, coordinates health research, and provides breeder referrals. Regional clubs across the United States host field trials, hunt tests, and specialty shows that allow Gordon enthusiasts to compete and connect. These events serve dual purposes: they evaluate breeding stock for working ability and provide venues for owners to learn from experienced handlers.

Within the Gordon Setter world, some tension exists between show-line and field-line breeders, similar to divisions seen in other sporting breeds. Show-line Gordons are bred primarily for conformation to the breed standard's physical specifications, emphasizing structure, coat quality, and movement in the show ring. Field-line Gordons, including springset lines, prioritize hunting performance, drive, and competitive field trial ability. Dogs from these different breeding programs can look and act quite differently, though both remain true Gordon Setters. Prospective buyers should understand these distinctions and select puppies from lines that match their intended purpose - a competitive field trial dog needs different qualities than a family companion who occasionally hunts.

The Gordon Setter community also includes rescue organizations that rehome Gordons who need new families due to owner circumstances, behavioral issues, or dogs from puppy mills and backyard breeders. Organizations like Gordon Setter Rescue coordinate across regions to evaluate, rehabilitate, and place dogs in appropriate homes. Adopting an adult Gordon can be an excellent option for experienced dog owners who understand the breed and can work with any existing behavioral challenges. These rescue groups provide valuable services while educating the public about the realities of Gordon Setter ownership and the importance of selecting dogs from responsible sources.

Resources and Continued Learning

Learning about Gordon Setters shouldn't end with reading websites or talking to a single breeder. Prospective and current owners benefit from engaging with multiple information sources to develop a complete understanding of the breed. The Gordon Setter Club of America publishes educational materials, health surveys, and breeding guidelines that provide valuable insights. Their website offers breeder directories, upcoming event calendars, and connections to regional clubs where you can meet Gordon owners and see dogs in various activities.

Books about Gordon Setters and sporting dog training provide deeper knowledge than online articles can offer. Titles covering bird dog training methods, positive reinforcement techniques for independent breeds, and sporting dog health management belong on every Gordon owner's bookshelf. Online forums and social media groups dedicated to Gordon Setters allow owners to share experiences, ask questions, and troubleshoot challenges. However, evaluate online advice critically - not everyone posting has accurate knowledge, and what works for one dog may not suit another.

Attending dog events offers irreplaceable learning opportunities. AKC field trials and hunt tests allow you to observe Gordon Setters in action, speak with breeders and handlers, and understand what well-trained dogs can accomplish. Many clubs welcome spectators and some offer mentorship programs pairing newcomers with experienced Gordon enthusiasts. For those interested in hunting with their Gordon, joining organizations like Pheasants Forever provides access to hunting lands, conservation efforts, and communities of upland bird hunters who appreciate versatile hunting dogs. The more you engage with the Gordon Setter community and sporting dog world, the better equipped you'll be to provide your Gordon with the life this breed deserves. The American Veterinary Medical Association pet owner resources provide evidence-based guidance on dog health, behavior, and responsible ownership applicable to Gordon Setters.
